What is DevSecOps?

The DevOps movement was not started from a single manifesto, instead it grew from a desire to improve on the status quo. For many years development and operations teams operated as seperate isolatated teams. This meant developers would not understand the needs of a production environment and operations staff would not trust software produced by developers.

The goal was to break down the barriers between teams so developers would push early versions into production environments and operations staff would be able to provide feedback. Operations staff could then bring their tooling into development so staff used a consistent set of tools. This helped reduce the software development lifecycle as software didn't need to be repackaged and by being involved early in development operations staff were more confident in continiously deploying the software into production.

This has meant production requirements have moved upstream and become development requirements. One of the common constraints on operations staff were quality assurance processes. This lead to development teams incorporating tooling to find code vulnerabilities, demonstrate appropriate code coverage and automate the quality assurance process. The increased focus on software quality improved the trust Operations staff have in the software they deploy.

Operations staff also have to work with Information Security (INFOSEC), the environments they create, the means to deploy software, etc.. need to be vetted and approved by information security. Many DevOps teams have developed software which did not meet information security requirements and had to be extensivily refactored. This lead it involving information security in all parts of the software development lifecycle and so the term DevSecOps was born.
