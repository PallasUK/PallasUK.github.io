Agile

If your looking at making the switch to agile or standing up a new team, making agile work for you can be challanging. We are here to help you decide in Scrum or Kanban is the correct choice, to provide guidance on story points and how they enable capacity planning. We can even work with business leaders to understand Agile metrics so you can known when a team is high performing and when intervention is required.

Agile is an iterative approach to product development which aims to deliver each progressive step within a project. Agile aims to reduce the risk of development by reducing the time between requirements gathering to delivery and acceptence.

The Agile Manifesto outlines a set of principles, Scrum and Kanban are approaches which allow teams to implement the Agile principles. Simply following Scrum or Kanban does not mean a team has achieved the princples or is reaping the benfits of Agile. This is even one of the pricinples people over process.

Lastly many organisations have multiple software development teams operating and these teams can have dependencies on each other. Scaled Agile frameworks (SAFe, Lean-Agile, etc..) have been developed to allow the management of these dependencies.

What can we do to help?

Pallas has expearence in managing a variety of Agile teams from small research projects to large support teams, applying Scrum and Kanban where appropriate, bringing that expearence to help tailor an approach that will work for you.

We understand that Agile is a set of principles and not processes and that sometimes achieving the ideal is not possible. The key thing is use the metrics agile provides to understand where things are going wrong and try different approaches until we find one that works for your teams.

  • Agile Kanban/Scrum coaching
  • Extracting and using metrics
  • Establish delivery pipelines
  • Integration of assurance into delivery
